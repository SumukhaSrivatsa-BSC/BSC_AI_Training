# Transformers & AI Application Architecture - MCQ Assessment

## Overview

Today I completed a 60-question assessment covering the fundamentals of AI Application Architecture and Transformer-based models.

The assessment was divided into eight sections, covering both conceptual understanding and real-world scenarios.

## What I Learned Today

### AI Application Architecture
- The five layers of a production AI application pipeline:
  - Input Layer
  - Orchestration Layer
  - Retrieval & Tools Layer
  - Safety & Quality Layer
  - Observability Layer
- Why input validation is the cheapest place to stop bad requests.
- How RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) helps AI systems answer using enterprise knowledge.
- The role of multi-model routing in balancing cost, speed, and quality.
- Importance of observability through cost, latency, and evaluation metrics.

### Transformers Fundamentals
- GPT models generate text by predicting the next token repeatedly.
- Difference between static and contextual embeddings.
- How tokenization, embeddings, and positional encoding prepare text for transformers.
- Difference between training and inference.
- Query, Key, and Value (QKV) attention mechanism.

### Understanding Transformers
- The 7-step transformer pipeline.
- Why positional embeddings are necessary.
- How self-attention captures relationships between words.
- Benefits of multi-head attention.
- How feed-forward layers refine token representations.
- Why modern LLMs use dozens or even hundreds of transformer blocks.

### Evolution of Transformers
- Limitations of Seq2Seq architectures.
- How Bahdanau and Luong Attention improved translation models.
- How ConvS2S introduced parallelization with convolutions.
- Why the Transformer architecture was revolutionary.
- Differences between BERT and GPT.
- Challenges of large language models such as computational cost and hallucinations.

### Scenario-Based Learning
- Applying AI architecture concepts to real-world business problems.
- Selecting appropriate models based on use cases.
- Understanding retrieval systems for enterprise chatbots.
- Managing quality, safety, and governance in AI applications.

## Key Takeaway

> Not every problem requires the largest model. Effective AI systems combine smart architecture, retrieval, safety controls, observability, and the right model for the task.
